Restaurants

Kanawa:


Kanawa Restaurant in Tokyo's Shibuya district offers an authentic Japanese dining experience. Known for its traditional cuisine and minimalist ambiance, it showcases seasonal ingredients in expertly crafted dishes, from sushi to tempura. With attentive service and a refined atmosphere, Kanawa invites guests to enjoy the essence of Japanese cuisine in style.


Okonomi Mura:

Okonomai Mura, located in Osaka, specializes in Okonomiyaki, a savory Japanese pancake grilled to perfection. With a cozy atmosphere and friendly staff, guests can enjoy watching their meal being cooked right before their eyes. Whether you're new to Okonomiyaki or a seasoned enthusiast, Okonomai Mura offers an authentic taste of Osakan cuisine in a memorable setting.




HOTELS

Grand Prince Takanawa:


The Grand Prince Hotel Takanawa in Tokyo's Minato ward offers a luxurious retreat for travelers seeking comfort and convenience. With modern design and impeccable service, the hotel features spacious rooms with stunning views, gourmet dining options, and state-of-the-art facilities including a fitness center and swimming pool. Nightly rates at the Grand Prince Hotel Takanawa typically range from $200 to $400, depending on the room category and availability. Whether guests are exploring the city or unwinding in the hotel's serene surroundings, the Grand Prince Hotel Takanawa provides an exceptional experience in the heart of Tokyo.



Hyatt Regency Tokyo:

The Hyatt Regency Tokyo Hotel, nestled in Tokyo's bustling Shinjuku district, offers guests a luxurious retreat with modern design and impeccable service. With spacious rooms boasting panoramic city views, upscale amenities, and diverse dining options, including Japanese and international cuisines, guests can indulge in a memorable culinary experience. Nightly rates at the Hyatt Regency Tokyo Hotel typically range from $250 to $500, depending on the room category and season. Whether exploring Tokyo's cultural attractions or unwinding in the hotel's tranquil ambiance, the Hyatt Regency Tokyo promises an unforgettable stay in Japan's dynamic capital




Monuments

Mount Fuji:

Japan's iconic peak, rises 3,776 meters above sea level on Honshu Island. Revered for its perfect cone shape and cultural significance, it attracts climbers and admirers alike. The climbing season from July to September offers unforgettable experiences, with sunrise vistas from the summit drawing visitors from around the world. Whether scaling its heights or admiring from afar, Mount Fuji promises a captivating encounter with Japan's natural and spiritual essence.



Tokyo Imperial Palace:

The Tokyo Imperial Palace, situated in the heart of Tokyo, stands as a symbol of Japan's rich heritage. Serving as the residence of the emperor, it features elegant architecture, serene gardens, and the iconic Nijubashi Bridge. While access to the inner grounds is limited, guided tours offer insight into its historical significance. A visit to this historic landmark provides a glimpse into Japan's imperial legacy and cultural traditions. The inner grounds of the palace are generally not open to the public. Only on January 2 (New Year's Greeting) and February 23 (Emperor's Birthday)
